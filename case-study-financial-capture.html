The Global Data Challenge

Commercial banks rely on standardized financial statements to feed downstream risk models. However, the source documents vary wildly depending on the region and the client type.

We successfully deployed our "Glass Box" capture engine across three distinct financial environments:

  • DUCA Financial (Canada): Handling complex Canadian tax returns (T2), Notices of Assessment, and bilingual (English/French) statements.
  • Alrahma (Micro-Credit): Ingesting informal, non-standardized ledgers and handwritten income statements from unbanked entrepreneurs.
  • BSIC (Benin): Managing OHADA-compliant accounting records and diverse West African financial formats.

The Breakthrough: Cognition vs. Computation

We solved this by separating the workflow into two distinct engines. Generative AI captures and reasons. Deterministic engines calculate and validate. This ensures we get the flexibility of LLMs to read diverse formats without the risk of math hallucinations.

The 5-Step Process

  • Faithful Capture (Lossless)
    The first agent captures the statement exactly as presented. If the source has 20 fields, we capture 20. No assumptions, just pure ingestion.
  • Cognitive Reclassification
    Specialized agents perform the expert-level reasoning. They combine equivalent items (e.g., Cash + Reserves), exclude non-pertinent assets based on bank policy, and map every element to the standardized structure.
  • Cross-Agent Consistency
    Multiple agents perform the tasks independently and cross-check each other. This allows the system to measure confidence and flag edge cases for human review only when necessary.
  • Deterministic Calculation
    Crucial: We do NOT use GenAI for math. Once the data is standardized, a traditional calculation engine computes ratios (Leverage, Liquidity, EBITDA). This eliminates calculation drift and regulatory risk.
  • Automated Risk Scoring
    The output is fed directly into internal risk models and benchmarked against industry data (Sector, Capital Intensity, etc.).

Why This Matters

This is not OCR. This is the industrialization of financial expertise. We captured how expert analysts think about reclassification and formalized it into a machine-speed workflow that is fully traceable for auditors.
