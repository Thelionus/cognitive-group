The Challenge: The "Silo" Trap

A leading North American financial institution with a large commercial portfolio faced chronic inefficiencies. Despite having strong front-office expertise, the loan origination workflow was fragmented across three isolated functions:

  • Account Managers: Client interaction and initial prep.
  • Middle Office (Risk): Credit analysis and structuring.
  • Operations (Back Office): Documentation, offer letters, and security.

Each loan application bounced back and forth 2-3 times between these groups. Account managers were spending 120 minutes per loan just on preparation, with half that time wasted on rework. Previous attempts to use Robotic Process Automation (RPA) had failed because the upstream data was too messy.

The Solution: Cognitive Orchestration

Instead of replacing their core systems, the bank deployed the Cognitive AI Workbench as an orchestration layer. This wasn't a standard LOS (Loan Origination System); it was an intelligent layer designed to think like a credit officer.

How the AI Workbench Works

  • Ingestion: Captures client docs, bank statements, and Equifax reports directly.
  • Normalization: Automatically standardizes variable financial statements (e.g., mapping different "Revenue" line items to a single standard).
  • Deterministic Analysis: Runs credit risk policies against the data to generate a structured, audit-ready loan request.

The Results: Frictionless Origination

The impact was immediate. By ensuring data was structured and validated before it ever reached the Operations team, the bank eliminated the primary failure mode of their previous automation efforts.

Operational Impact

  • Speed: Account Manager prep time dropped from 120 minutes to 10 minutes.
  • Accuracy: 100% elimination of back-and-forth iterations between Front Office and Risk.
  • Automation: Because the AI output was clean, the bank could finally turn on end-to-end automation for offer letters and security documentation.

The Proof of Concept proved that the AI Workbench alone—without a traditional LOS overhaul—was sufficient to modernize the commercial lending workflow.
