The Legacy Problem

Modern banks are trapped in systems designed decades ago. The cost is staggering.

01

Legacy Foundations

Most banks depend on decades-old core systems. Hard to modify, fragile, and maintained by a shrinking pool of experts. This limits modernization, scalability, and integration.

02

Patchwork Architecture

Dozens or hundreds of disconnected systems layered around the core. Complex integrations that frequently break. Inconsistent data models and fragmented information.

03

Extremely High Costs

Modern cores (Temenos, Flexcube, FIS, Fiserv, Sopra) require $1M+ just to start. Multi-year implementations with high licensing and support fees. Smaller institutions cannot afford enterprise-grade systems.

04

Difficult Configuration

Thousands of parameters, proprietary scripting, rigid data models. Everyday changes require deep expertise and long testing cycles. Creates permanent dependency on integrators.

05

Poor User Experience

Unintuitive interfaces, slow workflows, inconsistent modules. High training time and operational errors. Excessive friction for both employees and customers.

06

Locked Out Institutions

Small banks and credit unions forced to adopt low-cost offshore or ERP-based solutions. Results in weak data quality, poor reliability, limited compliance capabilities, and complex operations.
