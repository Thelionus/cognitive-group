History

Born from Research

The Cognitive Group (CG), headquartered in Montreal, was founded in 1995 by the Montreal Computer Research Institute (CRIM) to develop a Cognitive Engineering consulting practice.

Building on deep expertise in cognitive ergonomics and system design, we spent over a decade collaborating with Royal Bank of Canada (RBC) to optimize their loan origination processes. This foundation of "Tier-1 rigor" allowed us to develop BusinessBANKER.

Our Philosophy

Human-Centered, Trustworthy AI—Built as a Product.

Our philosophy begins with a fundamental belief: human expertise is a structured asset. Expert judgment is not intangible intuition—it can be observed, decomposed, formalized, and engineered into systems.

01. Cognitive Design

From Human Expertise to System Design

We apply Cognitive Task Analysis (CTA) to enter the decision-maker’s mental model. We capture how experts perceive situations and manage uncertainty, encoding human reasoning itself, not just correlations.

02. Co-Designers

Collaboration Over Vendor Dynamics

We operate as co-designers of intelligence. We work collaboratively with domain experts to construct systems that reflect real-world decision processes—not abstract theoretical models.

03. Product First

From Consulting to Product

We made a deliberate shift to become a fully product-oriented company. Our mission: eliminate bespoke configuration and reduce setup effort to near zero. Human expertise is captured once, then industrialized.

04. Performance

Excellence by Design

Our products are built with a non-negotiable standard: deterministic performance, high availability, and enterprise-grade speed. Intelligence is only valuable if it is dependable under pressure.

05. Auditability

Trust Is Not Claimed—It Is Proven

Every result must be explainable. Every discrepancy must be traceable. We do not hide uncertainty—we surface it. Trust is a core requirement, not a marketing claim.

06. Glass Box

Radical Transparency

From the first interaction, our systems are designed to be transparent by default. Users can see exactly which rules, evidence, or reasoning paths were used to reach a conclusion.
