01 | The Problem

The Brain Drain & The 90% Inefficiency

We collaborated with a major Canadian financial institution employing 300 adjudicators. Our initial analysis revealed a critical inefficiency: Adjudicators spent 90% of their time on routine verification and only 10% on actual decision-making.

More dangerously, the bank was facing a "Brain Drain." The intuition required to assess complex loans resided in the minds of senior adjudicators who were nearing retirement. This expertise was undocumented, making the bank vulnerable to inconsistencies and errors.

"I have to get out of the Bureau, go back in here, go back in there. I'm flipping back and forth constantly. It's insane."
— Senior Adjudicator
02 | The Approach

Real Intelligence Capture

We didn't just build a chatbot; we cloned the institutional memory of the bank. Using Cognitive Task Analysis (CTA), we performed a deep extraction of the "Gold Standard" logic.

We interviewed the bank's most senior experts, challenging them to verbalize the specific variables driving their intuition. We mapped 100 years of collective lending expertise into a deterministic code structure.

This allowed us to build a pre-trained "Mortgage Agent" that doesn't guess—it reasons exactly like a senior adjudicator.

By automating the capture and verification of client information—such as identity, income, and property valuation—we minimized the risk of errors. Once information is verified, the application of bank policy is consistent, traceable, and instant.

03 | The Results

Deterministic Reliability

The implementation fundamentally shifted the operating model of the lending unit.

  • Expertise Encapsulated: The bank now possesses a digital asset containing its best decision-making logic, immune to staff turnover.
  • Time Savings: Automating the verification layer reduced the processing time per application by up to 90%.
  • Cost Reduction: By streamlining the workflow and reducing manual error correction loops, the client realized an estimated $20 million in operational savings.
  • Risk Management: The AI enforces strict policy adherence (e.g., checking environmental questionnaires for >1 acre properties), ensuring loans are granted only to qualified applicants.
